Why executives are moving beyond chatbots

Executives want delegation, not prompts

Major publications note that senior leaders are experimenting with AI “twins” and agents to absorb meetings, inbox triage, and operational busywork — not just drafting text [nytimes.com].

Computer use is the inflection point

Google’s Gemini 3.5 Flash now includes built‑in computer use, underscoring that real value comes when agents can see screens, click, and complete multi‑step work [blog.google], [forbes.com].

Repetition exposes cost and friction

Daily executive workflows — logging into dashboards, filing reports, scheduling, approvals — repeat endlessly. Without memory, each run costs the same time and money.

Security and realism matter

Once agents operate computers, risks follow. Analysts highlight prompt‑injection and guardrail challenges as this category matures [searchenginejournal.com], [thenextweb.com].
